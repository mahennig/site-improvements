Georgiana Gogolan Internal Review

Site Audit

Review Findings
georgianagogolan.com

A comprehensive content, design, SEO and performance audit of the live site and editorial journal.

Date: 22 May 2026 Version: v2 — extended Files reviewed: index.html + 7 articles + CSS/JS

Overall Verdict

The site has a strong visual direction — premium, coherent, editorial and memorable. The personal brand reads clearly, and Designing La Dolce Vita has real potential to become both an SEO and brand asset.

From an SEO standpoint there are several concrete blockers reducing organic visibility:

  • 1Sitemap is incomplete and has outdated lastmod dates.
  • 2Bilingual site runs on a single URL with client-side JS switching — not crawlable as two distinct pages.
  • 3Homepage and portfolio are image-heavy but thin on crawlable text.
  • 4Editorial section has good content but is not strongly enough connected internally.
  • 5Images are unoptimised — 87 files totalling ~97 MB, no WebP or srcset, individual files up to 7 MB.

What is working well ✓

🎨

Visual direction

Premium, editorial aesthetic. Playfair + DM Sans, dark + lime palette — distinctive and on-brand.

🔍

SEO foundations

<title>, meta description, canonical, OG, JSON-LD, robots.txt and sitemap all present.

✍️

Editorial content

Journal articles are long enough to index. Niched topics (Milan Design Week, brand spotlights, interviews).

♿

Accessibility

Skip link, ARIA labels, aria-expanded, aria-live, prefers-reduced-motion.

⚙️

Technical foundation

Framework-free HTML/CSS/JS. Cache headers and gzip via .htaccess. llms.txt present.

A — Content

A1

Homepage reads beautifully but says too little for search

High impact

The portfolio is almost entirely visual. Projects have evocative names but no dedicated pages, and there is no descriptive text about project scope, client types, deliverables, location or outcomes. Google cannot rank the page for intent queries like "architect Bucharest" or "interior designer Romania".

Recommendations

  • Add 1–2 paragraphs about services offered, project types and geography.
  • Turn key projects into individual crawlable pages.
  • Include natural phrasing: "architect in Bucharest", "residential interiors", "boutique commercial spaces".
A2

Designing La Dolce Vita has strong potential as a content hub

Medium impact

The hub page has a good title, H1, article listings and a Blog schema — a solid starting point. However, there is very little original introductory text, limiting its ability to compete for relevant queries.

"Designing La Dolce Vita is Georgiana Gogolan's editorial series about architecture, interior design, Milan Design Week and the emotional experience of space."

Recommendation

Add ~150–250 words of descriptive intro text below the H1 explaining what the series is, why it exists, and who it is for.

A3

Language inconsistency in the journal

Medium impact

Six articles are in English; the newest (mihai-gurei-interview/) uses <html lang="ro">. This creates a mixed editorial experience and complicates any multilingual SEO strategy.

Choose one approach

  1. Keep the journal in English; give Romanian articles their own alternate URL.
  2. Separate EN and RO onto distinct paths with proper hreflang.
  3. Publish everything in one language until resources allow full localisation.
A4

Inconsistent brand naming

Low impact

The section name alternates between Designing La Dolce Vita and Designing la Dolce Vita across titles, nav links, breadcrumbs and schema markup. Choose one official casing and apply it everywhere.

A5 ✱

Authorship inconsistency between articles

Medium impact

Articles carry two different authors in OG metadata and JSON-LD: Georgiana Gogolan on four articles, Andreea Pohrib on three. Andreea has no visible presence on the frontend (no byline, no bio). Her author JSON-LD object also lacks a url field.

Recommendations

  • Decide whether to credit co-authors visibly (byline, bio card).
  • Standardise JSON-LD — all author objects must include a url.
  • If Andreea ghost-writes, attribute all articles to Georgiana for brand consistency.
A6 ✱

llms.txt is missing the newest article

Low impact

site/llms.txt lists 6 articles; mihai-gurei-interview/ is absent. Update llms.txt every time a new article is published.

A7 ✱

Featured journal card uses lazy loading above the fold

Medium impact

All article card images in designingladolcevita/index.html use loading="lazy" — including the large featured card visible above the fold on most screens. This delays the LCP image.

Fix

Set loading="eager" on the first (featured) card image only.

B — Design / UX

B2

Portfolio looks interactive but leads nowhere

High impact

Portfolio items are div blocks — not links. Users expect to click through to project pages. Google also sees far less depth than it would with dedicated URLs for each project.

Recommendation

Create individual project pages with images, description, role, location, year, services and brief process notes. Add a "View project" CTA on each card.

B5

Legal / trust inconsistency in forms and footer

High impact

The newsletter form has a GDPR consent checkbox. The homepage contact form does not. Critically, the homepage footer has no Privacy Policy link at all — even though every other page includes one.

Recommendations

  • Add a short privacy note + link near the contact form.
  • Add a Privacy Policy link in the homepage footer-bottom section.
B6

No explicit image dimensions in HTML

Medium impact

All <img> elements lack width and height attributes, risking CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) on slow connections. Add dimensions, or ensure wrappers have aspect-ratio set in CSS.

B7 ✱

All forms share one Formspree endpoint

Low impact

Contact enquiries and newsletter signups from all 8+ pages post to https://formspree.io/f/mojpqvnd. They are only separated by a source field — there is no inbox-level routing.

Recommendation

Create a second Formspree endpoint for the newsletter, or configure labels/filters within the account.

B8 ✱

Inline JS duplicated across all article pages

Medium impact

Each article page re-implements navigation, scroll reveal and newsletter form logic inline — the same code that already exists in main.js. Any fix must be applied in 8+ places manually.

Recommendation

Move shared logic into main.js (or a new blog.js) and load it via <script src> on article pages.

B9 ✱

Inline <style> blocks on journal pages

Low impact

Each journal page contains an inline <style> block of 100–160 lines. Styles are not separately cacheable and are partially duplicated across pages. Move them into a shared blog.css.

C — SEO

C1

Sitemap is incomplete and has stale modification dates

Very high priority

site/sitemap.xml is missing https://georgianagogolan.com/designingladolcevita/mihai-gurei-interview/. Also, lastmod on designingladolcevita/ shows 2026-04-23 even though the section has been updated since. An incomplete sitemap slows Googlebot's crawl discovery.

C2

Multilingual implementation is not SEO-friendly

High impact

All three hreflang tags on the homepage point to the same URL (/). Google sees one page, not two. Language is switched via client-side JS — no second indexable URL is produced for the Romanian version.

If separate EN/RO visibility is the goal

  • Create distinct URL paths, e.g. / (EN) and /ro/ (RO).
  • Add proper hreflang cross-references between them.
  • Set canonical tags appropriately on each version.
C5

Structured data good but inconsistent between articles

Medium impact

All articles use BlogPosting + BreadcrumbList — correct schema types. Field completeness varies: some have richer JSON-LD, others are minimal. Author objects are inconsistent (url present on some, absent on others).

Standardise template to always include

headline, description, image, author (with url), publisher, datePublished, dateModified, inLanguage, mainEntityOfPage, BreadcrumbList.

C8

Internal linking is thin

Medium impact

The journal is linked from the nav and a few homepage locations. More is needed to distribute authority and guide users.

  • A "Featured from Designing La Dolce Vita" block on the homepage.
  • Related article links at the bottom of each post.
  • Contextual links from articles to relevant services or contact.
C10

Invalid markup in the Mihai Gurei article

Medium priority

Line 287 of mihai-gurei-interview/index.html contains an unclosed <p> tag — the paragraph ends with a <div class="img-trio"> at line 290 without a closing </p>. Quick fix; validate with an HTML validator after.

C11 ✱

Massively unoptimised images — the single biggest performance risk

Critical — do first
Total image weight~97 MB across 87 files
Heaviest portfolio image4.1 MB — project-cielo-aperto.jpg
Heaviest article image7.3 MB — talenti-8.png
Images in WebP/AVIF0 of 87
Images with srcset0 of 87

A single homepage load can transfer 30–40 MB in images alone. Core Web Vitals (LCP, FCP) will be significantly affected. Google uses CWV as a ranking factor.

Recommendations — do these first

  1. Convert all images to WebP (JPG fallback via <picture>).
  2. Resize: portfolio images ≤ 1,600 px wide; article images ≤ 1,200 px wide.
  3. Compress: JPG @ 80–85%; WebP @ 75–80%.
  4. Add srcset variants at 480w, 960w and 1,600w.
  5. Targets: homepage < 3 MB total; per article < 2 MB.
C12 ✱

OG tags incomplete on sub-pages

Low impact

Homepage has a full OG set. Journal and article pages are missing og:image:width, og:image:height and og:image:alt. Social media previews may be poorly formatted on LinkedIn, Facebook and WhatsApp.

C13 ✱

No theme-color meta and no web app manifest

Low impact

Adding <meta name="theme-color" content="#111111"> would give mobile browsers an integrated address bar colour. A site.webmanifest improves Android home screen integration. Minor, but quick wins.

C14 ✱

No language switcher on journal or article pages

Low impact

The EN/RO toggle on the homepage disappears entirely in the journal and articles. If the journal is intentionally English-only, add a small note such as "available in English only" to set expectations.

D — Performance

D1

CSS — single file of ~58 KB (uncompressed)

Low priority

style.css bundles every component's styles including those only used on specific pages (MDW series, blog cards). Every page downloads and parses all of it. Acceptable now, but splitting into base.css + blog.css would help in the long term.

D2

Font loading — well configured ✓

Google Fonts with preconnect + display=swap is correct practice — avoids FOIT (Flash of Invisible Text). Consistent across all pages. No action needed.

D3

JS — lightweight, but duplicated on article pages

Low impact

main.js (~14 KB) + i18n.js (~18 KB) = ~32 KB total. With gzip, real transfer is ~10–12 KB — excellent for a framework-free site. The only issue is the inline JS duplication across article pages (see B8).

Bottom Line

Design

Very strong, distinctive and memorable. A genuine editorial identity that stands out.

Content

Good in tone and brand voice, but the homepage and portfolio are too image-heavy and text-light for effective SEO.

SEO

Foundations are in place, but several concrete gaps need to be resolved for Designing La Dolce Vita to gain meaningful organic visibility.

Performance

The most urgent problem is image weight (~97 MB total, files up to 7 MB). This directly impacts Core Web Vitals and therefore search rankings.

Designing La Dolce Vita already has the right editorial hub format — but for better organic visibility it urgently needs an updated sitemap, optimised images, consistent naming, stronger internal linking, and a more SEO-friendly multilingual strategy.
