Personal Brand Hub Strategy

May 29, 2026 Strategic blueprint 9 sections

This document defines the strategy for restructuring georgianagogolan.com from an interior-design studio site into a personal brand hub — positioning Georgiana first, introducing her three professional worlds, and routing visitors to the right destination.

Section 01

Executive Summary

The problem

The current site positions Georgiana as a studio owner and interior designer. It duplicates content that belongs on spacecasuals.ro, buries her personal voice, and ignores two of her three professional worlds entirely. It also contains a factual error — labeling her as "Co-founder" of Space Casuals.

The solution

Rebuild georgianagogolan.com as a personal editorial hub:

  • Georgiana first — her philosophy, voice, and story lead
  • Three worlds second — each brand gets one concise section
  • Selected proof on-site — enough to build trust, not a full archive
  • Deep detail routed outward — visitors go to brand sites only when ready

georgianagogolan.com answers "Who is Georgiana?" first, and only then guides visitors to "where they should go next."

Section Specifications

5.1 Hero

Purpose: establish identity instantly.

  • One-line identity statement — no paragraph
  • Current strong visual can remain
  • CTA should feel editorial, not studio-sales

Designing spaces, stories, and brands.

Avoid: long hero paragraphs, competing claims, studio language.

5.2 Intro Statement

Purpose: make her roles scannable in 2 seconds.

Recommended update

Architect.
Interior Designer.
Content Creator.
Partner, Space Casuals · Founder, Design Mindset Agency.

5.3 About / Positioning

Purpose: explain her point of view before introducing any brand.

Must include:

  • Architecture background: hospitals, embassies, diplomatic buildings, office towers
  • The shift toward intimacy of interior spaces
  • The home as a conscious, identity-shaping environment
  • One strong quote block

"A truly beautiful space is not just an image, but a continuous and sincere expression of your identity."

"Un spațiu cu adevărat frumos nu e doar o imagine, ci o expresie continuă și sinceră a identității tale."

Credential line:

100+ interior projects 20+ brand partnerships Milano Design Week ISH Frankfurt The Woman 2025

5.4 Three Worlds

Purpose: make the business model understandable in under 20 seconds.

Each block follows the same anatomy: label → headline → 1–2 sentences → CTA → visual.

Architecture and interior design — from first sketch to finished space.

Working with residential and commercial clients on projects that balance architectural rigour with deeply personal interior decisions.

See the full portfolio → spacecasuals.ro

Exploring the places, brands and ideas that define how we live beautifully — through a YouTube series, a journal, and design fair coverage.

5.5 Selected Work

Purpose: prove she designs real spaces, without duplicating Space Casuals.

  • 3–5 projects maximum (down from current 14)
  • Only projects where Georgiana had clear authorship
  • Short narrative framing, not just a grid
  • CTA to spacecasuals.ro for the full archive
  • Note that the Space Casuals site is Romanian-only

This section becomes the English-language front door to her Space Casuals work, since spacecasuals.ro is Romanian-only.

Section 07

What to Keep vs. Remove

Keep ✅

  • ✅ Dark editorial visual identity
  • ✅ Bilingual EN/RO system
  • ✅ Strong hero visual treatment
  • ✅ Journal + social integration
  • ✅ Brand marquee (updated)
  • ✅ Contact form with Formspree

Remove or replace ❌

  • ❌ 14-item portfolio mosaic → 3–5 curated
  • ❌ Services section → Three Worlds
  • ❌ Process timeline (01–05 steps)
  • ❌ "Co-founder" → "Partner"
  • ❌ JSON-LD founder claim
  • ❌ "Let's start a project" → "Let's connect"

One person with multiple expressions — not three disconnected companies awkwardly stacked together.

Section 09

Final Recommendation

Restructure georgianagogolan.com as a personal editorial hub:

  • Georgiana first — her story, philosophy, and voice lead everything
  • Three worlds second — each brand gets one concise, visually strong section
  • Selected proof on-site — enough to build trust, not to overwhelm
  • Deep detail offloaded — only when the visitor is clearly ready

The site should feel like meeting Georgiana at a design event — warm, confident, articulate, and clearly connected to multiple creative worlds — not like browsing a studio portfolio.
